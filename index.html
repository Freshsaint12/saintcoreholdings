Operating Thesis

Five beliefs that decide what we build — and what we don't.

01

Software should feel inevitable.

Not configurable. Not "powerful." Obvious. If a partner or a student or a homeowner has to think about the tool, the tool has failed.

02

Ship the workflow, not the feature list.

Professionals don't buy checkboxes. They buy their Tuesday back. We build for the week somebody actually has, not the one a screenshot captures.

About

A Chicago holding company. Founder-led. Privately held.

Saint Core Holdings was founded in 2024 by Solomon Amoah. We exist because the tools that run professional work — tax prep, clinical study, home ownership — are either built for a decade ago or built for nobody in particular.

We don't take outside capital. We don't spin up a product unless we'd use it daily. We ship from the database up and we stay responsible for the result.
